If $\sqrt{2}$ is in $\mathbb{Q}$, then $\left|\sqrt{2}\right|$ is in $\mathbb{Q^+}$¶

($\sqrt{2} \in \mathbb{Q^+}$ as well by convention, but this proof is really about showing that no squared rational number is equal to $2$.)
